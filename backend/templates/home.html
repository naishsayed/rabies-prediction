About Rabies

What is Rabies?

Rabies is a deadly viral disease that affects the central nervous system, often transmitted through animal bites.

Symptoms

Fever, headache, excess salivation, muscle spasms, paralysis, and mental confusion.

Prevention

Immediate wound cleaning, vaccination, and avoiding stray animal contact can prevent rabies.

Treatment

Once symptoms appear, rabies is nearly always fatal, but vaccines can prevent the onset.

Transmission

The virus is mainly transmitted through the saliva of infected animals via bites or scratches.

High-Risk Animals

Common carriers include dogs, bats, raccoons, skunks, and foxes. Unvaccinated domestic animals are at risk.

Post-Exposure (PEP)

PEP involves wound washing, a dose of human rabies immune globulin, and a series of rabies vaccines.

Global Impact

Rabies causes tens of thousands of deaths annually, mostly in Asia and Africa. It is 99.9% fatal but also 100% preventable.
